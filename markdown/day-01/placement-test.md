## Part B — Reading (1 text, 5 questions, 15 points)

### Read the text and choose A, B, C, or D.

**Studying Abroad**

More and more students choose to study abroad for one or two semesters. Living in another country helps them improve their language skills and become more independent. However, studying abroad can also be challenging. Students may feel homesick, especially during the first weeks. They must adapt to a new culture, different food, and unfamiliar academic systems.

Universities often offer support services for international students, including language courses and social events. Many students say that despite the difficulties, the experience is valuable for their future careers. Employers often look for candidates who have international experience and can communicate in English.

**26.** What is the main idea of the text?
- A) All students should study abroad.
- B) Studying abroad has benefits and challenges.
- C) Employers do not value international experience.
- D) Language courses are unnecessary abroad.

**27.** According to the text, students abroad may feel ______ at first.
- A) angry
- B) homesick
- C) bored
- D) famous

**28.** Universities help international students by ______.
- A) paying for their flights
- B) offering support services
- C) finding jobs for them
- D) teaching only local languages

**29.** The word **"adapt"** in paragraph 1 is closest in meaning to ______.
- A) refuse
- B) adjust
- C) forget
- D) travel

**30.** What do many employers look for?
- A) Candidates with no English
- B) Candidates who never travel
- C) Candidates with international experience
- D) Candidates who study only one subject
