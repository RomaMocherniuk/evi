# Grammar — Modals & Passive Voice

Murphy **Intermediate** Units 3–4, **Elementary** Units 11–12, 21–22

## Part A — Modals

| Modal | Meaning | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| can / can't | ability, permission | I can swim. |
| could | past ability, polite request | Could you help me? |
| must | strong obligation (speaker) | You must register online. |
| have to | external obligation | Students have to pay a fee. |
| don't have to | no obligation | You don't have to attend. |
| mustn't | prohibition | You mustn't cheat. |
| should | advice | You should rest before the exam. |
| may / might | possibility | It might rain. |

### Gap drill

1. You ______ register before the deadline. It's the rule. (must / mustn't / can't)
2. She ______ speak three languages when she was ten. (can / could / must)
3. We ______ wear a tie; it's optional. (mustn't / don't have to / should)
4. This ______ be the right answer — I'm not sure. (must / might / have to)
5. You ______ smoke here. (mustn't / don't have to / wouldn't)

**Key:** 1-must or have to  2-could  3-don't have to  4-might  5-mustn't

## Part B — Passive Voice

**Form:** be + past participle

- Present Simple: is/are + done
- Past Simple: was/were + done
- Present Perfect: has/have + been + done

### Active → Passive

- They **built** the bridge in 1990. → The bridge **was built** in 1990.
- People **speak** English here. → English **is spoken** here.

### Gap drill

6. The exam ______ (hold / holds / is held) every June.
7. My application ______ (sent / was sent / has sent) yesterday.
8. All tasks ______ (must finish / must be finished / must finishing) in 45 minutes.
9. The results ______ (will announce / will be announced / are announcing) next week.
10. English ______ (speak / is spoken / spoke) in many countries.

**Key:** 6-is held  7-was sent  8-must be finished  9-will be announced  10-is spoken
