**Time:** 15 minutes

## Text — Environment

Climate change is one of the most serious problems (1)______ world faces today. Scientists have warned for decades that temperatures (2)______ rise if we continue burning fossil fuels. Many governments have now promised to reduce emissions, but critics say the plans are not ambitious (3)______.

Individuals can also make a difference. Using public transport instead (4)______ driving alone cuts pollution. Eating less meat and wasting (5)______ food helps too. Some people think their actions are too small to matter, (6)______ experts argue that collective change starts with personal habits.

Renewable energy has become (7)______ (cheap) than coal in many regions. Solar panels and wind turbines (8)______ (install) across Europe at a record pace last year. However, developing countries often need financial support (9)______ they can switch to clean energy quickly.

The next decade will be decisive. If we (10)______ (not act) now, future generations will face more extreme weather and rising sea levels.

| Gap | A | B | C | D |
|-----|---|---|---|---|
| (1) | a | an | the | — |
| (2) | will | would | rise | risen |
| (3) | enough | too | very | such |
| (4) | of | from | than | to |
| (5) | fewer | less | little | few |
| (6) | because | although | so | if |
| (7) | cheaper | cheapest | more cheap | cheap |
| (8) | were installed | installed | were installing | have installed |
| (9) | before | after | until | unless |
| (10) | don't act | won't act | didn't act | not act |

**Note for (7):** insert **cheaper** from context (than coal).
