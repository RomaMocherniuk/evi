## Text — Media and Society

Journalism has changed dramatically in the twenty-first century. Traditional newspapers (12)______ fewer copies than they did twenty years ago, but online news sites (13)______ millions of readers every day. Some journalists worry that speed is now more important (14)______ accuracy.

Social media allows anyone to share information instantly. However, not everything that goes viral is true. Experts advise readers to check (15)______ least two reliable sources before believing a shocking headline. Critical thinking has become (16)______ essential skill for modern citizens.

Television remains popular, although young people increasingly prefer streaming platforms. Documentaries and drama series produced for the web often explore social issues (17)______ were rarely shown on mainstream channels. Subtitles help viewers improve their foreign language skills while they watch.

Advertising pays for much of the content we consume free of charge. Companies track our preferences and show personalised ads, (18)______ some users find disturbing. Privacy laws have been tightened in several countries, yet many people still do not read the terms they agree (19)______ when they install an app.

Education programmes now teach media literacy in schools. Students learn to identify bias, recognise propaganda, and write balanced essays. Teachers say these lessons help pupils become more responsible (20)______ they grow up.

If schools (21)______ not invest in digital skills, graduates may struggle in the job market. Employers expect candidates to communicate clearly online and to use collaboration tools. A recent survey found that graduates who completed internships were (22)______ (likely) to find work within six months.

Looking ahead, artificial intelligence may write simple news reports, but human editors will still be needed to verify facts and (23)______ ethical decisions. The best journalists combine curiosity with integrity. They remind us that a free press supports democracy (24)______ giving citizens the information they need.

Finally, readers should remember that consuming news is not the same (25)______ understanding it. Taking time to reflect on what we read—rather than reacting immediately—leads to better public debate. This habit is worth cultivating, (26)______ life is busy and notifications constantly demand our attention.

Many universities now offer master's programmes in communication studies. Applicants must demonstrate strong reading skills and a good command (27)______ English. The ЄВІ is one way to prove that command, (28)______ it focuses on reading and language use in context.

Candidates who prepare systematically usually perform better than those who rely (29)______ luck. They practise timed tasks, review their mistakes, and build vocabulary (30)______ topic. Success is possible even for learners who start at A2, provided they work consistently for several weeks.
