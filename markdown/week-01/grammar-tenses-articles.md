# Grammar — Tenses & Articles in Context (Week 1)

Use with Murphy **English Grammar in Use Elementary** Units 1–2, 5–6, 11–15.

---

## Part 1 — Tense identification drill

Choose the best form.

1. She usually ______ (walk / walks / is walking) to university.
2. Listen! The neighbours ______ (argue / are arguing / argued) again.
3. I ______ (don't see / didn't see / haven't seen) him since Monday.
4. While I ______ (studied / was studying / have studied), my phone rang.
5. They ______ (will finish / finish / are finishing) the project tomorrow.

**Key:** 1-walks  2-are arguing  3-haven't seen  4-was studying  5-will finish / are finishing

---

## Part 2 — Articles (a / an / the / —)

1. He wants to be ______ engineer.
2. ______ Moon is visible tonight.
3. I need ______ advice about my thesis.
4. We met at ______ bus stop near ______ cinema.
5. ______ Ukraine is a country in Eastern Europe. (exam style: often **—**)
6. She plays ______ violin in ______ orchestra.

**Key:** 1-an  2-The  3-—  4-the / the  5-—  6-the / an

---

## Part 3 — Mixed gapped sentences (exam style)

Choose A, B, C, or D.

**(1)** My brother ______ in Kyiv since 2019.
- A) lives
- B) is living
- C) has lived
- D) lived

**(2)** This is ______ most interesting book I have read this year.
- A) a
- B) an
- C) the
- D) —

**(3)** We must ______ the results before Friday.
- A) find out
- B) find
- C) finding
- D) to find

**(4)** If you ______ water every day, you feel healthier.
- A) drink
- B) will drink
- C) drank
- D) would drink

**(5)** The conference ______ by over 200 people last month.
- A) attended
- B) was attended
- C) has attended
- D) is attending

**(6)** She is ______ taller than her sister.
- A) more
- B) most
- C) much
- D) many

**(7)** There ______ many opportunities for graduates who speak English.
- A) is
- B) are
- C) was
- D) be

**(8)** I look forward ______ you at the conference.
- A) see
- B) seeing
- C) to see
- D) to seeing

**Key:** 1-C  2-C  3-A  4-A  5-B  6-C  7-B  8-D

## Teacher notes

- Q1: since → Present Perfect
- Q4: Zero conditional (general truth)
- Q8: look forward **to** + -ing (common gap type)
