# Task 2 — Multiple Choice Practice (формат ЄВІ)

**Instructions:** Read the questions first. Then read the text. Choose A, B, C, or D.  
**Time:** 12 minutes per text.

---

## Strategy reminder

1. Read **questions** before the text
2. Read text once — look for **paraphrase**, not identical words
3. Eliminate 2 wrong answers
4. Beware: if the same word appears in text and answer — often a **trap**

---

## Practice Text 1 — Remote Work

The number of people working from home has increased dramatically since 2020. Many employees enjoy flexible hours and no daily commute. However, managers report that teamwork can suffer when colleagues rarely meet face to face. Some companies now require staff to come to the office two or three days per week—a model called «hybrid work».

Experts suggest that hybrid arrangements may become standard in many industries. Workers save time and money, while employers can reduce office costs. Still, not every job suits remote work; nurses, factory workers, and shop assistants must usually be present on site.

**1.** What is the main topic of the text?
- A) Why all jobs should be done from home
- B) Changes in how and where people work
- C) How to become a factory worker
- D) The history of offices before 2020

**2.** According to the text, one disadvantage of working from home is that ______.
- A) employees never save money
- B) teamwork may become weaker
- C) everyone must work seven days a week
- D) managers always work from home

**3.** The word **«commute»** in paragraph 1 refers to ______.
- A) travelling to and from work
- B) cooking lunch at home
- C) writing emails
- D) sleeping late

**4.** «Hybrid work» means ______.
- A) working only at night
- B) combining home and office days
- C) working without a computer
- D) retiring early

**5.** Which jobs are mentioned as usually requiring physical presence?
- A) Lawyers and teachers
- B) Nurses and shop assistants
- C) Writers and artists
- D) Pilots and drivers

## Practice Text 2 — Learning Languages

Adults often believe they are too old to learn a foreign language, but research shows that motivation matters more than age. Regular practice—even fifteen minutes a day—can lead to noticeable progress. Watching films with subtitles, reading simplified news, and speaking with friends are all useful strategies.

Language exams, such as those required for university admission, test reading and grammar in context rather than oral skills. Candidates who understand the exam format and practise timed tasks tend to perform better than those who only memorise word lists.

**6.** What does the text suggest about adult learners?
- A) They cannot learn new languages
- B) Motivation is more important than age
- C) They should avoid watching films
- D) They learn faster than children always

**7.** How much daily practice is mentioned as helpful?
- A) Five hours
- B) At least fifteen minutes
- C) Only on weekends
- D) None is necessary

**8.** University language exams mainly assess ______.
- A) speaking and listening only
- B) reading and grammar in context
- C) handwriting speed
- D) knowledge of one word list

**9.** The word **«noticeable»** is closest in meaning to ______.
- A) invisible
- B) easy to see or observe
- C) illegal
- D) boring

**10.** Successful exam candidates often ______.
- A) never use a timer
- B) practise tasks in exam conditions
- C) ignore the exam format
- D) study only grammar rules without reading
