## Second Conditional

**If + Past Simple, would/could + infinitive** — unreal present/future

If I **knew** more phrasal verbs, I **would feel** more confident.

**Gap:** If they ______ (have) more time, they ______ (apply) to two programmes.
→ **had / would apply**

**Exam trap:** Do not use **would** in the if-clause: If I **would know** ❌

---

## Used to / Would (past habits)

- **used to** + infinitive — past state or habit (not now)
- **would** + infinitive — repeated past actions (often with «when I was young»)

I **used to live** in a small town.  
My grandmother **would tell** us stories every evening.

**Gap:** There ______ a library in our village, but it closed in 2010.
→ **used to be**

---

## Reported Speech

| Direct | Reported |
|--------|----------|
| Present Simple | Past Simple |
| will | would |
| can | could |
| am/is/are | was/were |

She said, «I **am** ready.» → She said she **was** ready.  
He told me, «I **will** call you.» → He told me he **would** call me.

**Gap:** The professor said the exam ______ (be) difficult but fair.
→ **was**

---

## Relative Clauses — exam focus

**Defining (essential):** The book **that/which** I borrowed is due tomorrow.  
**Non-defining (extra info, commas):** My thesis supervisor, **who** works in Berlin, is very helpful.

**Where** for places: the university **where** I studied  
**Whose** for possession: the student **whose** paper won

### Mixed gaps (10)

1. If I ______ you, I would start preparing earlier. (be)
2. She ______ to drink coffee every morning. (use)
3. He said he ______ finished the chapter. (have)
4. The article ______ was published last week is relevant. (which/who)
5. Dr Smith, ______ office is on the third floor, teaches linguistics. (whose/which)

**Key:** 1-were  2-used  3-had  4-which  5-whose
