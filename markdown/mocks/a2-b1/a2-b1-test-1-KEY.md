## Task 3

| Gap | Answer | Explanation |
|-----|--------|-------------|
| (12) | B — are considering | Present continuous for current consideration/plans |
| (13) | C — are offered | Passive voice: scholarships are offered by universities |
| (14) | A — will | First conditional: "if you apply early, you will stand…" |
| (15) | C — are taught | Passive voice: courses are taught in English |
| (16) | A — linguistically | Adverb modifying "prepare": prepare linguistically |
| (17) | C — living | "living abroad" — gerund as subject |
| (18) | B — are formed | Passive: friendships are formed |
| (19) | A — their | Referring back to "students" (plural pronoun) |
| (20) | A — valued | "highly valued" — passive adjective |
| (21) | A — study | Second conditional: "If you study abroad…" (hypothetical) |
| (22) | B — would | Second conditional result clause |
| (23) | A — first | "in the first weeks" — ordinal number |
| (24) | B — make | "make an effort" — fixed collocation |
| (25) | A — meet | Parallel infinitives after "get involved": joining, attending, meet |
| (26) | B — are available | "support services are available" — passive/adjective complement |
| (27) | A — yourself | "investment in yourself" — reflexive pronoun (informal register) |
| (28) | A — are | Present simple: "difficulties are manageable" |
| (29) | A — choose | Present simple fact: "thousands of students choose" |
| (30) | A — was | Past simple: looking back at the completed experience |

> **Note on gap (21)–(22):** The context could be read as first conditional or second. Given "if you studied abroad" (hypothetical), second conditional (B — would) is preferred for (22). For (21), "If you study" (first conditional, more direct) is also valid — both interpretations are common in B1 texts. Accept both A/A or A/B depending on your target.

## Teacher Notes

- Main grammar tested: **passive voice** (present simple and past), **conditionals** (1st and 2nd), **gerunds as subjects**, **collocations** (make an effort, highly valued)
- Key phrasal/lexical focus: "make an effort", "stand out", "get involved", "living abroad", "are available"
- Common A2–B1 errors: forgetting passive auxiliary (are), mixing conditional types, using adjectives instead of adverbs (linguistic vs linguistically)
