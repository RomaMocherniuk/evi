## Task 2 — Multiple Choice (Questions 7–11)

### Text: The Rise of Remote Work

Remote work, once considered a privilege of a few technology companies, has become mainstream across many industries. Since 2020, millions of employees worldwide have experienced working from home, and many are reluctant to return to a traditional office full-time. For employers and employees alike, the shift has revealed both opportunities and challenges.

Productivity studies produce conflicting results. Some research suggests that remote workers complete more tasks per day because they have fewer interruptions and no commute. Others argue that creativity and collaboration suffer when people do not meet face to face. The reality likely depends on the nature of the work — tasks that require deep focus benefit from quiet home environments, while brainstorming sessions and team projects often work better in person.

For many employees, the greatest benefit of remote work is flexibility. Being able to attend to personal responsibilities — a doctor's appointment, a school event, a family matter — without using annual leave is highly valued. Studies show that flexible workers report higher job satisfaction and are less likely to resign.

Companies face different pressures. Attracting talent in competitive markets often requires offering remote or hybrid options. However, maintaining company culture, onboarding new staff, and monitoring performance are more complex when teams are distributed across multiple locations.

**7.** What has happened to remote work since 2020?
- A) It has decreased because offices reopened.
- B) It has become more common across many industries.
- C) It has been banned in most countries.
- D) Only technology companies use it.

**8.** What do some studies suggest about remote workers' productivity?
- A) They are always less productive than office workers.
- B) They make more phone calls.
- C) They complete more tasks because they have fewer interruptions.
- D) They need more supervision.

**9.** When does in-person work have an advantage, according to the text?
- A) For tasks that need deep concentration.
- B) For teamwork and brainstorming.
- C) For answering emails and reports.
- D) For all types of tasks equally.

**10.** What do flexible workers report, according to studies?
- A) Higher salaries.
- B) Less interest in their jobs.
- C) Higher job satisfaction.
- D) More frequent promotions.

**11.** What challenge do companies face with distributed teams?
- A) Employees refuse to use technology.
- B) It is harder to maintain culture and onboard new staff.
- C) Remote workers earn too much.
- D) Offices become too expensive to rent.
