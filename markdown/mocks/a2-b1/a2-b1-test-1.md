## Task 2 — Multiple Choice (Questions 7–11)

### Text: Young People and the Environment

Young people today are more aware of environmental issues than any previous generation. Climate change, plastic pollution, and biodiversity loss are topics they encounter daily — in school, on social media, and in the news. Many feel a deep sense of responsibility to act, even when governments and businesses appear slow to respond.

In several countries, student-led campaigns have achieved notable results. Pressure from young activists has led some city councils to ban single-use plastics in public spaces, invest in cycling infrastructure, and commit to carbon-neutral targets ahead of official deadlines. These victories show that grassroots action can influence policy at the local level.

However, environmental concern alone does not always translate into changed behaviour. Research suggests that young people who are most worried about climate change are not necessarily those who reduce their own carbon footprint the most. Convenience, cost, and social norms continue to shape everyday choices.

Educators argue that combining emotional awareness with practical skills is the most effective approach. Teaching young people how to calculate their carbon footprint, choose sustainable products, and engage with local decision-makers may produce more lasting change than simply raising alarm.

**7.** According to the text, why do young people feel responsible for environmental issues?
- A) Because older generations do not care at all.
- B) Because they encounter these topics everywhere in daily life.
- C) Because schools force them to campaign.
- D) Because they own more businesses.

**8.** What have student-led campaigns achieved in some cities?
- A) They have replaced governments completely.
- B) They have caused some councils to ban plastics and invest in green infrastructure.
- C) They have forced companies to close factories.
- D) They have created new national laws.

**9.** What does research suggest about young people's behaviour?
- A) The most worried students always have the smallest carbon footprint.
- B) Young people never recycle or reduce waste.
- C) Concern about climate change does not automatically lead to greener choices.
- D) Young activists live the most sustainable lives.

**10.** According to the text, what continues to shape everyday choices?
- A) The advice of environmental scientists.
- B) Convenience, cost, and social norms.
- C) Official government campaigns.
- D) The opinions of young activists.

**11.** What do educators believe is the most effective approach?
- A) Banning all social media use in schools.
- B) Only discussing the emotional side of climate change.
- C) Combining emotional awareness with practical skills.
- D) Making students pay for their own carbon footprint calculations.
