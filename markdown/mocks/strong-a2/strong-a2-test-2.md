## Task 1 — Matching (Questions 1–6)

Read texts 1–6. Match each text with the most suitable heading (A–H).
There are **two extra headings** you do not need.

**A)** A service that helps people manage personal finances

**B)** A community project to improve public spaces

**C)** An opportunity for young people to develop leadership skills

**D)** A programme offering support for mental wellbeing

**E)** A notice about changes to parking in the area

**F)** An event raising money for a local charity

**G)** A scheme promoting healthy eating habits

**H)** A course to help adults re-enter the job market

---

**1.** Mind Matters is a new drop-in service at the community centre offering free, confidential conversations with trained counsellors. No appointment is needed, and the service is open every weekday from 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Anyone aged 16 and over is welcome to attend.

**2.** Starting next month, the council will close Maple Street to cars during school hours in order to create a safer route for children walking to school. A temporary one-way system for local residents will be in place during the closure period.

**3.** Citywide volunteers are needed this October to plant trees, install new benches, and repaint outdoor play equipment in parks across the district. The project welcomes individuals, families, and corporate groups. Sign up at volunteernow.org or call the parks department.

**4.** The Saturday Farmers' Table initiative encourages local families to cook one meal per week using seasonal, unprocessed ingredients. Free recipe cards are available at participating supermarkets, and the scheme is linked to a national campaign to reduce processed food consumption among families.

**5.** North Bridge Community College is offering a twelve-week "Back to Work" programme for adults who have been out of employment for more than a year. The course covers CV writing, digital skills, and interview techniques. There is no fee for participants who are receiving certain government benefits.

**6.** Our annual sponsored walk through the national park raises thousands of pounds for the local children's hospice each year. This year's route is 18 kilometres long. Participants can register online and collect sponsorship forms from any local pharmacy.

## Task 2 — Multiple Choice (Questions 7–11)

### Text: Teenagers and Screen Time

A recent study looked at how much time teenagers spend on screens each day. On average, young people aged 13 to 18 spend about seven hours in front of a phone, tablet, or computer — not counting time used for homework. Researchers found that this habit has both benefits and disadvantages.

On the positive side, technology helps teenagers stay connected with friends and family. Many use social media to share experiences, join interest groups, and support each other. Educational apps and online tutoring platforms have also made learning more accessible for students in rural areas.

However, experts warn that too much screen time, especially before bed, can disrupt sleep patterns. Poor sleep affects concentration, mood, and overall health. Some teenagers reported feeling anxious or unhappy after spending long periods on social media, particularly when they compared themselves to others online.

Specialists recommend balancing screen time with outdoor activities, face-to-face socialising, and regular sleep routines. Setting daily screen limits and taking short breaks every hour can make a significant difference.

**7.** How many hours of screen time do teenagers typically have per day, according to the study?
- A) About three hours.
- B) More than ten hours.
- C) About seven hours.
- D) Exactly five hours.

**8.** What is one positive effect of technology for students in rural areas?
- A) They can buy cheaper phones.
- B) Learning becomes more accessible through online platforms.
- C) They do not need to go to school anymore.
- D) They can watch more television.

**9.** What problem can too much screen time before bed cause?
- A) It makes teenagers talk too much.
- B) It disrupts sleep patterns.
- C) It improves concentration.
- D) It reduces screen brightness.

**10.** How did some teenagers feel after using social media for a long time?
- A) Relaxed and happy.
- B) Bored and uninterested.
- C) Anxious or unhappy.
- D) Confident and motivated.

**11.** What do specialists recommend to improve the situation?
- A) Banning all screens for teenagers.
- B) Using only educational apps.
- C) Balancing screen time with outdoor activities and regular sleep.
- D) Reducing homework to make more time for screens.
