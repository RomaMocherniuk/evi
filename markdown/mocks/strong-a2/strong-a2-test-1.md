## Task 2 — Multiple Choice (Questions 7–11)

### Text: Learning a New Language as an Adult

Many adults decide to learn a new language after the age of twenty-five. Some do it for career reasons, others for travel or personal interest. Research shows that adult learners can progress very quickly when they use the right methods and stay consistent.

One of the most effective approaches is called "comprehensible input." This means listening to or reading material that is slightly above your current level — not too difficult to understand, but challenging enough to teach new vocabulary. Podcasts, graded readers, and films with subtitles are popular tools for this.

Another key factor is daily practice. Even fifteen minutes a day produces better results than a three-hour session once a week. Forming a habit of listening, speaking, or writing a little every day trains the brain to process the language automatically.

Adult learners sometimes worry that they will never sound like native speakers. However, having a strong accent does not stop communication. Clarity and confidence matter more than a perfect accent.

**7.** Why do many adults start learning a new language?
- A) Because their school makes them.
- B) For career, travel, or personal reasons.
- C) Because children cannot learn languages.
- D) To pass a children's test.

**8.** What is "comprehensible input"?
- A) A very difficult grammar textbook.
- B) Material that is too easy and boring.
- C) Material slightly above your current level.
- D) Listening to native speakers only.

**9.** What does the text say about daily practice?
- A) A three-hour weekly session is the best approach.
- B) Fifteen minutes every day is more effective than one long session.
- C) Adults should practise only at weekends.
- D) Short sessions are useless for adults.

**10.** Why do some adult learners feel worried?
- A) Because they are afraid of exams.
- B) Because they think they cannot travel.
- C) Because they worry about never sounding like natives.
- D) Because they cannot afford language classes.

**11.** According to the text, what matters more than a perfect accent?
- A) Studying grammar every day.
- B) Clarity and confidence.
- C) Using very formal language.
- D) Memorising a dictionary.
