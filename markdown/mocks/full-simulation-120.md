## Task 2 — Multiple Choice (7–11)

### Text: Digital Privacy

Every click and search leaves traces online. Companies collect data to personalise advertisements, while governments may request information for security reasons. Privacy advocates argue that citizens should know what is stored and how long it is kept.

New regulations give users more control in some regions. They can download their data or ask for deletion. However, many people accept lengthy terms and conditions without reading them. Education campaigns encourage users to review settings regularly and use strong passwords.

For students, privacy also matters academically. Plagiarism detection systems and learning platforms store submissions. Universities publish policies explaining how this information is used. Understanding these rules helps learners protect their work and reputation.

## Task 3 — Gapped Text (12–30)

Higher education in Europe continues to attract international master's candidates. Universities advertise programmes in English to reach a global audience, and many applicants welcome the chance to study abroad. However, admission often depends (12)______ standardised tests that measure reading and language use.

The ЄВІ assesses whether candidates can understand authentic texts and select appropriate grammar and lexis in context. It does not test speaking or listening, (13)______ written comprehension remains central. Preparation therefore emphasises timed reading and gap-fill practice.

Candidates who begin early usually perform better than those who cram. A structured plan might include diagnostic testing, weekly grammar in context, vocabulary by topic, and regular mock exams. An error log helps learners avoid repeating the same mistakes, (14)______ makes review sessions efficient.

Reading tasks require different strategies. Matching exercises reward quick identification of main ideas. Multiple-choice questions often use paraphrases, so students must not choose answers merely because the same word appears in the text and the option. That pattern is a common (15)______.

Gap-fill sections test collocations, phrasal verbs, verb forms, articles, and prepositions. Relative clauses and participle structures appear in harder items. Even A2 learners can improve significantly if they focus on high-frequency patterns (16)______ than attempting to master every advanced rule.

Time management is critical. Spending half the session on six matching items leaves too little time for nineteen gaps. Students should practise skipping difficult questions and returning later. On the real exam, unanswered items earn no points, (17)______ educated guessing after elimination is rational.

The ТЗНК block evaluates general academic competence through verbal and analytical tasks. Minimum scores apply separately to each block, so strong English cannot compensate for ignoring ТЗНК entirely. At least two full practice sessions help candidates understand its logic and pacing.

Motivation fluctuates during intensive preparation. Teachers can maintain morale by celebrating incremental gains—a higher mock score, a faster reading time, a phrasal verb used correctly. Progress may feel slow day to day, (18)______ cumulative effort produces visible results over weeks.

Peer support also helps. Study partners who share strategies and hold each other accountable often stay consistent. Nonetheless, copying answers without understanding undermines learning. True improvement requires analysing why an option was wrong and which rule applies.

Before exam day, candidates should simulate full conditions at least once. They ought to rest adequately, prepare documents, and plan travel to the testing centre. Anxiety is normal, (19)______ preparation reduces it. Trusting the plan matters as much as last-minute cramming.

After the exam, results appear in the applicant's online account. Whatever the score, the preparation period strengthens skills needed for master's study—reading articles, writing emails, participating in seminars. Some candidates retake the test, applying lessons from their first attempt. Persistence and reflection characterise many success (20)______.

Ultimately, the ЄВІ is a gatekeeper, not a definition of worth. It measures specific skills under time pressure. With thirty-five days of focused work, an A2 learner can reach scores sufficient for admission to many programmes. The key is consistency, format familiarity, and honest error analysis (21)______ wishful thinking.

Good luck to every candidate. Read carefully, manage time wisely, and answer every question. You have prepared systematically; now demonstrate what you have learned. Your future master's cohort awaits students who worked diligently to meet this (22)______.
