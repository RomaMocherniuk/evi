## Teacher Notes — B1–B2 Error Patterns

- **Gap (15):** "infer" (listener draws a conclusion) vs "imply" (speaker hints at something) — classic B2 vocabulary distinction
- **Gap (18):** Non-defining relative clause: "a manager who delivers feedback indirectly" — do not use "delivering" here as it changes the register
- **Gap (20):** Passive in relative clause: "a comment that is considered routine" — students often write "a comment that considers routine"
- **Gap (24):** "a willingness to + infinitive" — noun form required after article "a"
- **Gap (30):** Gerund after preposition: "not about abandoning" — students often use "abandon" (bare infinitive) incorrectly

## Task 1 Strategy Reminder

In EVI-style matching tasks at B1–B2:
1. Identify the **main idea** of each paragraph — do not match on surface keywords
2. Be alert to **paraphrase**: headings use different words than the texts
3. Eliminate the two extras **last**, not first
4. Text 5 (consumer protection) and Text 6 (urban mental health) are the most commonly confused with extras B and E — anchor on the **regulatory / policy** angle for text 5 and the **social** angle for text 6
