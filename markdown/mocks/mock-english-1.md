## Task 2 — Multiple Choice (7–11)

### Text: International Students

Studying in a foreign country can be exciting and stressful at the same time. International students must adapt to new academic expectations and often write longer essays than they wrote at home. Many universities offer writing centres where tutors help students structure arguments and cite sources correctly.

Culture shock is common during the first semester. Students may miss familiar food, language, and social routines. Joining clubs and attending language exchange events can ease loneliness. Research shows that students who build local friendships report higher satisfaction and better grades.

Financial planning is also important. Tuition, rent, and transport vary widely between cities. Some students take part-time jobs, although visa rules may limit working hours. Scholarships and assistantships can reduce costs for master's candidates.
