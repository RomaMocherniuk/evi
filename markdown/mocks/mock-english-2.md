Parks and green roofs improve life in crowded cities. Trees reduce air pollution and lower temperatures in summer. Residents who live near parks often exercise more and report lower stress. However, maintaining green spaces costs money, and some argue that land should be used for housing instead.

City planners try to balance these needs. Community gardens allow neighbours to grow vegetables together, which builds social ties. Studies in several European capitals show that property values near well-maintained parks tend to rise over time, which can benefit local budgets through taxes.
